Frequently asked

The questions
everyone asks.

If your question isn't here, ask it in the form below — fastest way to a real answer.

Do I own the code and the site when you're done?
Yes — fully. Code repository, hosting account, domain, content. You can take it to any other engineer or agency and they'll be able to pick it up. No vendor lock-in, no hostage situations.
What if I need changes after launch?
Small tweaks within 30 days are on me — typos, color adjustments, copy fixes. Bigger changes are billed at a flat rate or rolled into a small monthly retainer if you want me on call. No surprise hourly bills.
How fast can you start?
Usually within a week of the scoping call. Starter and Business websites typically ship in 48 hours to a week from kickoff. Automation and bot projects depend on scope — I'll give you a firm timeline in the proposal.
What if my project doesn't fit your published tiers?
That's most projects, honestly. The published tiers cover the common cases. Anything else — custom site, larger automation, internal tool, full product build — gets scoped on a 30-minute call and quoted as a fixed-price proposal. No vague hourly estimates.
Do you handle hosting and ongoing maintenance?
For sites: yes, hosting is included or I'll set you up on your own account so you own it. For automation: I deploy to whatever infrastructure makes sense (yours or mine), and document everything. Ongoing maintenance is a small monthly retainer if you want me on call — or zero if you're comfortable owning it.
Can you work with my existing team or agency?
Yes — most of my work plugs into something that already exists. I write clean code, document what I build, and communicate directly with whoever is responsible for the system on your end. No politics.
What if I'm not happy with the work?
The proposal locks scope and deliverables in writing before any code is written. If something ships that doesn't match the proposal, I fix it. If you change your mind on direction mid-project, we either re-scope or stop — your call, no drama.
