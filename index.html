// The straight answers

Everything people actually ask about the price.

Why are the prices posted publicly?
Because you shouldn't have to book a call to find out if you can afford someone. Every tier is a real base price with real add-ons. When you send a brief, you already know the ballpark — the call is about scope, not sticker shock.
Do I actually own the code?
Yes — full ownership transfers at final payment. You get the repo, the domain access, the deploy pipeline, and every credential. If we part ways down the road, another developer can pick up right where I left off. That's non-negotiable.
What if I need something that isn't in the add-on list?
Ask. The add-on list covers 90% of what site owners need — anything outside gets a clean line-item quote on the same call. If it's small (under an hour), it usually gets folded into the build for free. If it's big, you'll see the number before I write a line of code.
Can I upgrade my care plan later?
Anytime, both directions. Start on Hosting Only at $50/mo, upgrade to +4 hrs the month you launch a marketing push, drop back down when you're done. Care plans are month-to-month — no annual lock-in.
What if my site breaks?
If it broke because of code I wrote, the fix is free — full stop. Same-day response on Signature and Flagship tiers, next-business-day on Basic. If it broke because a third party (your DNS host, a plugin, an API you're using) changed something, the fix comes out of your care hours or gets quoted separately.
Do you require a contract?
For builds under $1,500, a one-page email quote is enough. Everything above gets a proper SOW — scope, deliverables, timeline, payment schedule, revision cap. 50% deposit to start, 50% at launch (three-way split for Flagship). It protects both of us.
Can I pay in installments?
Payment is milestone-based, not time-based: 50% deposit, 50% at launch on Basic and Signature. Flagship splits into three: 40% deposit, 20% at design approval, 40% at launch. That way you're never paying for work that hasn't been done yet.
What's the deal with the rush fee?
+35% if you need the site in half the normal timeline. That's what covers the nights and weekends I'd be putting in to hit it. Basic delivered in 5 days instead of 10, Signature in 2 weeks instead of 4. Not always possible on Flagship — some builds just take the time they take.

Frequently asked

The questions
everyone asks.

If your question isn't here, ask it in the form below — fastest way to a real answer.

Do I own the code and the site when you're done?
Yes — fully. Code repository, hosting account, domain, content. You can take it to any other engineer or agency and they'll be able to pick it up. No vendor lock-in, no hostage situations.
What if I need changes after launch?
Small tweaks within 30 days are on me — typos, color adjustments, copy fixes. Bigger changes are billed at a flat rate or rolled into a small monthly retainer if you want me on call. No surprise hourly bills.
How fast can you start?
Usually within a week of the scoping call. Starter and Business websites typically ship in 48 hours to a week from kickoff. Automation and bot projects depend on scope — I'll give you a firm timeline in the proposal.
What if my project doesn't fit your published tiers?
That's most projects, honestly. The published tiers cover the common cases. Anything else — custom site, larger automation, internal tool, full product build — gets scoped on a 30-minute call and quoted as a fixed-price proposal. No vague hourly estimates.
Do you handle hosting and ongoing maintenance?
For sites: yes, hosting is included or I'll set you up on your own account so you own it. For automation: I deploy to whatever infrastructure makes sense (yours or mine), and document everything. Ongoing maintenance is a small monthly retainer if you want me on call — or zero if you're comfortable owning it.
Can you work with my existing team or agency?
Yes — most of my work plugs into something that already exists. I write clean code, document what I build, and communicate directly with whoever is responsible for the system on your end. No politics.
What if I'm not happy with the work?
The proposal locks scope and deliverables in writing before any code is written. If something ships that doesn't match the proposal, I fix it. If you change your mind on direction mid-project, we either re-scope or stop — your call, no drama.
